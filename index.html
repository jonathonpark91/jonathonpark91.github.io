Swaggerest

Platform for Users to share and post swag

Swaggerest is a platform I developed to be able to share and post pictures that I thought had swagger or that interested me. I developed it as my first application and it serves as an inspirational board for style.

For this project I focused on a clean, minimalist layout with responsive pictures that were searchable as well. I used a combination of Angular.js, jQuery, Bootstrap, Javascript, CSS, and HTML for the Front-End and used Firebase for the Back-End.

Tick-Get

Marketplace for users to buy, sell, or trade concert tickets

Tick-Get is a market place for users to buy, sell, or trade concert tickets. Tick-Get came to fruition as a result of our team's experiences in struggling to find quality buyers and sellers for concert tickets. In addition, we wanted a place where unwanted tickets could be used to barter for wanted tickets.

For this project, our team used HTML, CSS, jQuery, and Bootstrap for the Front-End and used Ruby on Rails and PostgreSQL for the Back-End.

Visit Tick-Get

HappyR Hour

Web App to quickly find the nearest happy hours!

Have you ever wanted to go have a beer or two after a long day at work? You probably tried to find the closest restaurants with happy hours but at the same time you were wasting valuable minutes into those happy hours. HappyR Hour was made so you could quickly and most efficiently find those happy hours and spend more of your valuable time enjoying those happy hour deals.

HappyR Hour was developed on the Front-End with HTML, CSS, Javascript, jQuery, Bootstrap, and on the Back-End using Ruby on Rails and PostgreSQL. Its features include a Facebook Login, Flash Messages/Pop-up Notifications, and use of the Google Maps API amongst other things.

Visit HappyR Hour

Stepping Stone

Platform for resume critiques and feedback

Have you ever wanted to apply to a job but had no real knowledge of how resumes were formatted for that industry? Our team found ourselves in this exact scenario and decided to make a social platform in which users could easy upload their resumes for friendly feedback from close friends and people knowledgable about that field.

For Stepping Stone, our team had the ambitious goal of self-teaching ourselves the MEAN Stack. This meant that we used primarily Angular.js for the Front-End and a combination of Node.js, Express, and Mongodb for the Back-End.
